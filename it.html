Expertise

By trusting in my services, you can expect utmost confidentiality, reliability and professionalism.

The mother-tongue principle:

I only translate into my mother tongue: English. This ensures that my translations are pleasing and easy to read, whether for a native audience or for international readers. In this way, I can go beyond simple literal translation and draw on a wealth of experience and a natural instinct for correct and clear usage. Moreover, given that I can work instinctively, translating only towards my mother tongue is simply quicker and easier, allowing me to provide higher-quality content at a more competetive price. However, if you do require translations into Italian or German, I'd be happy to put you in contact with a trusted member of my network of mother-tongue colleagues.

Skopos theory

Asides from the raw materials of terminology, style and domain knowledge, the translator's most important tool is an effective translation methodology that accommodates the text's purpose and context. One such methodology is so-called skopos theory, derived from the Greek for "aim". This presupposes that all words are not simply isolated, but interconnected in a web of meaning and context, all encapsulated in a text's purpose. It is this purpose that is the driving force of the text and which should serve as a compass to navigate through challenging translation decisions. Using this metholodogy gives me the confidence to treat the text holistically, allowing me to make adjustments and accommodate the target culture. This principle applies for the creative writing required in advertising as much as it does for the strict conventions of legal texts. It isn't only my goal to simply translate words, but to convey meaning and ultimately, allow the reader to truly understand the text and to help you achieve your aim.
As grammarian Wolf Schneider once quipped, "There's always one who has to make the effort: either the writer, or the reader." In my case, I pride myself on digging deep into the text and its genre in order to provide textually coherent translations that flow seamlessly in the mind of the reader.
